How we make that promise

Built around bearings that don't wear out.

At the center of every Gammill machine is a set of hydrodynamically lubricated journal bearings, with clear, non-staining oil delivered by a network of little hoses. Industrial equipment since the 1880s has relied on this type of bearing because of its durability, and this is an important part of why Gammill machines are widely hailed as "workhorse" machines.

What does wear out over decades is everything electrical - motors, electronics, controls, wiring. Even when those parts age out, the journal-bearing-driven head and the cast-iron table they sit in don't. You keep the bones. The rest is replaceable (this is explained in Ch 11).

Every Gammill built since 1997 is still in service today. No other longarm manufacturer can say that. It's also why pre-owned Gammills hold the highest resale value in the longarm industry.
