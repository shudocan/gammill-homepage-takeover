Why Gammill - Chapter 11

Retrofit,
not replace.

Other longarm makers expect their machines to age out and be replaced. Gammill machines are designed to be retrofit instead - bringing the very oldest to current specification, on the same head and table you already own. Reuse what lasts. Replace what wears out. Same machine. Same studio. Same owner.

Made to be modernized

Any Gammill, of any era, can be brought up to current spec.

A retrofit takes any Gammill from any era and modernizes it. We reuse the parts engineered to last forever - the cast-aluminum machine head and the welded tube-steel table - and replace everything that ages: motors, electronics, controls, wiring, software. The result is essentially a brand-new machine, on the bones of the one you already love.

There are three retrofit tiers, one per current model:

  • Vision 2 Retrofit - $9,999 - modernizes a hand-guided Gammill to current Vision 2.0 specification
  • Statler Retrofit - $26,999 - adds full Statler computerization to a hand-guided or older Gammill machine
  • Statler Ascend Retrofit - $33,999 - upgrades to the fastest and most precise current model

Every retrofit includes delivery, installation, and new-owner training on the modernized machine. The retrofit pricing is dramatically less than buying a new machine, and the result is the same: a current-spec Gammill in your studio.

No other longarm manufacturer offers retrofitting. None. When a competitor's machine gets old, the owner's only option is to scrap it and start over. That's why most longarm brands have a ten-year-ish useful life expectation - and why a thirty-year-old Gammill is still earning its keep in someone's studio today.
