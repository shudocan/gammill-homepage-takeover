Stitch quality isn't defined by stitch regulation or motor horsepower. It's about tension that stays put and a machine that moves effortlessly, as if it was reading your mind. Who wants to be ripping out stitching and inventing expletives?

What stitch quality is actually about

Tension that stays put. Movement that doesn't fight back.

Every modern longarm has stitch regulation and several different stitching modes. Of course the Gammill has the most functional and useful design - but let's not miss the forest for the trees. The two things that actually determine whether your stitching is satisfactory are tension stability and smooth movement.

Tension stability has always been difficult for longarm quilters. Most longarms use the same basic tension design as a domestic sewing machine - but longarm quilting is more demanding, and "okay" tension isn't going to satisfy you. In the earliest days of longarming, quilters noticed loops appearing on the back of the quilt after long days, especially on hot afternoons. Ken Gammill set his inventive mind to the problem and figured out (and patented) two exclusive mechanisms. Thirty years later, Intermittent Tension and Automatic Thread Escapement have come to define perfect stitching in the longarm world. Those dreaded afternoon loops? Unknown to Gammill owners.

Smooth movement is the downstream effect of everything you've already read - the investment-cast aluminum chassis, the welded tube-steel table, the Breeze Track system. The machine goes effortlessly where you want it to.

  • Intermittent Tension separates tension from the checkspring and clamps the top thread at key moments to prevent loops, eyelashes, and railroad tracks
  • Automatic Thread Escapement keeps tension steady through temperature and humidity changes by opening a small "door" for the thread to slip through
  • M-class large-capacity bobbin - about 2× standard capacity, fewer thread changes, with no stitch-quality penalty thanks to the patents above
  • The least-fussy tension in the industry - no accursed loops on the back
  • Smooth movement - downstream effect of the build quality of the machine chassis, table, and Breeze Track
  • Four stitch modes included (Stitch Regulate, Coast, Constant Speed, Baste) - table stakes, but standard

*Intermittent Tension, Automatic Thread Escapement, no timing belts, and the professional table are not available on 18-inch machines. 18" machines include a Home Pro Table.
