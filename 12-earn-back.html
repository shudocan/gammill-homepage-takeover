Why Gammill - Chapter 12

Do you want to earn back your investment?

Many Gammill owners are hobbyists - they buy a longarm for love, not money. But many also discover their Gammill can earn itself back, sometimes within 18 months, by quilting tops for other people. It's optional. But... the math works.

If quilting becomes your primary work and not just a side hustle: an established Statler longarmer finishes two to three queen-size edge-to-edge tops a day. At normal market rates that's $500-$600 per day, from a single machine. (The Ascend finishes 3-5 a day.)

Quilt shop owners who add a Statler Ascend report fabric sales 2-3x afterwards - because customers who only came in to drop off a quilt top are walking past the fabric to get to the drop-off counter.

The monthly payment is not the real cost.

Most longarm pitches obsess over the monthly payment. The real cost of any major purchase is depreciation - what you spent, minus what you got back when you sold it.
